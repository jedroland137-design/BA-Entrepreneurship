# Managerial Economics – COURSE_INFO

Course Code: DLBBWME01_E

---

## 1. COURSE OVERVIEW

This course introduces the core principles of microeconomics and their application in managerial decision-making. It focuses on how firms and consumers make decisions under scarcity and different market conditions.

---

## 2. CORE LEARNING STRUCTURE (UNIT MAP)

### Unit 1: Fundamentals of Economics
- Definition of economics
- Scarcity and choice
- Opportunity cost
- How economists think

### Unit 2: Supply, Demand & Market Equilibrium
- Demand and supply theory
- Market equilibrium
- Elasticity (price, income, cross)
- Applications in real markets

### Unit 3: Consumer Behaviour
- Utility theory
- Marginal utility
- Budget constraints
- Consumer optimisation
- Willingness to pay

### Unit 4: Production & Costs (Perfect Competition)
- Production functions
- Cost structures (fixed, variable, marginal)
- Short-run vs long-run
- Firm behaviour in perfect competition

### Unit 5: Market Structures (Imperfect Competition)
- Monopoly
- Monopolistic competition
- Oligopoly
- Pricing strategies and market power

### Unit 6: Game Theory & Strategic Behaviour
- Strategic decision-making
- Simultaneous games
- Sequential games
- Nash equilibrium (if included in course depth)

### Unit 7: Information & Behavioural Economics
- Information asymmetry
- Moral hazard & adverse selection
- Behavioural biases
- Real-world decision deviations
